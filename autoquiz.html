Auto Quiz

  • Front-end: Express (Node.js), Socket.IO, Bootstrap
  • Back-end: Flask (Python), Gunicorn
  • Hosting: Heroku
  • Multiple users can connect online using their own own device (desktop, laptop, mobile, tablet, etc.)

This project was completed in under 24 hours at the 2019 Facebook Hackathon in Sydney.

The idea behind Auto Quiz was to create an educational tool that could generate quizzes on any given topic using the Wikipedia API. Teachers would be able to generate fun quizzes on many different topics without having to put time into researching and creating the quiz.

Unfortunately, the results the Wikipedia API was too inconsistent for us to parse within the time limit of the hackathon so we had to find another API. In the end, we reduced our scope to just actors and used an API for IMDB.

Auto Quiz allows multiple users to connect to the same quiz and compete against each other.

The people taking the quiz click "Join". Multiple users can join by opening the site in a different tab or another device (mobile, tablet, laptop, etc.). They then enter the name they want displayed to other users and click "Submit".

The host clicks "Create" to create and run the quiz. They then enter the name of an actor, click "Submit", then wait for people to join the lobby. Once everyone has joined, they click "Start!".

The people taking the quiz will then be shows a question and 4 answers to choose from. They should click the one they think is correct.

After waiting for people to answer the question, the host can reveal the answer before moving onto the next question.

If the answer is revealed, the people taking the quiz will see the answer at the bottom.

When the quiz is done, the people taking the quiz will see their score and have the option of submitting it to the leaderboard for other participants to see.

After giving enough time for the participants to submit their scores, the host can click a button to publish the leaderboard to everyone.

The participants will then see the leaderboard on their screens.
